# Q10 — Price Tier Distribution Analysis

## Results

| Price Tier | Item Count | Tier Revenue (R$) | Avg Review |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| 1. Budget (<R$50) | 38,718 | 1,211,987.18 | 4.04 |
| 2. Mid (R$50-149) | 50,509 | 4,673,509.09 | 4.04 |
| 3. Premium (R$150-399) | 18,337 | 4,167,871.06 | 4.06 |
| 4. Luxury (R$400+) | 4,188 | 3,396,162.35 | 4.03 |

## Recommendations

Mid tier (R$50-149) dominates on both dimensions: highest item count (50,509) and highest revenue (R$4.67M / 35% of total GMV). This is the platform's core engine — protect and grow this segment before anything else. Any disruption to Mid tier seller supply or pricing would have the largest negative impact on overall platform revenue.

Luxury tier (R$400+) delivers the highest revenue density on the platform: 4,188 items generating R$3.4M — roughly R$811 revenue per item compared to R$92 per item in the Budget tier. Satisfaction holds at 4.03 proving logistics handles high-value orders competently. Each additional verified high-ticket seller added generates disproportionate GMV. Prioritize onboarding in watches_gifts and computers where premium demand already exists per Q9 results.

Budget tier (<R$50) is the efficiency problem: 38,718 items shipped for only R$1.21M — 9% of total GMV despite representing 35% of all items. Fulfillment cost per shipment is roughly equal regardless of item price meaning Budget orders consume logistics resources at a loss relative to their revenue contribution. Introduce a minimum order value threshold for free shipping to push Budget buyers toward adding items and crossing into Mid tier naturally.
